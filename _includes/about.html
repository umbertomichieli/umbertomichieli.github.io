About

I was born in Italy in 1994. I got my BS and MS respectively in Information Engineering (2016) and in Telecommunication Engineering (2018) from the University of Padova.
Currently I am a Ph.D. student at the Department of Information Engineering of the same University under the supervision of Pietro Zanuttigh.

My main research interest is the design of Deep Learning systems for the semantic segmentation task which can incrementally learn to segment and to label new classes over time. Furthermore I am focused on Unsupervised Domain Adaptation techniques for semantic segmentation which allows to train deep neural networks on large synthetic datasets, where the generation of the ground-truth is inexpensive, and then adapt them to real dataset without labels.
I am also passionate of link-prediction in Complex Networks and applications of Game Theory.

Check out the publications section to know more about these topics and the Multimedia Technology and Telecommunication Lab at University of Padova.
